White Couch

The Incident

Ever since the explosive incident, the white couch—and by extension, the living room—is considered Shrek's domain. The consequences of his mishap still haven't been fully quantified.

Re-synthesis

Shrek's aching body gave out, which gave way for something else to creep its way in. His autonomy had succumb to the brooding persistence of another soul, drifting together throughout the new house in a numb and anesthetic state. This necromantic impetus keeps them both alive, and confined to the interior of the new house (particularly, of course, the white couch). Today, they are essentially one entity, but they can still be perceived separately in exceptional or nebulous circumstances.

Barb

It's not clear what connected the spirit to Shrek in the first place. She (known as Barb or Barbus) is often hostile and was apparently unable to peacefully depart after death, even before the Day of Imbalance. When prominent, her manifestation appears as a pale-white, sickly young girl with tattered hair, bloodied teeth, lips and pressed eyes.
